  Problem Statement 1

AI-Based Prediction of High-Risk Plantar Pressure Zones for Prevention of Diabetic Foot Ulcers

PROBLEM CONTEXT

Diabetic Foot Ulcers (DFUs) are one of the most severe complications of diabetes mellitus. Due to peripheral neuropathy, many diabetic patients lose sensation in their feet and cannot detect injuries or excessive pressure while walking.

Chronic abnormal plantar pressure distribution leads to tissue breakdown and ulcer formation. Once ulcers develop, they are difficult to heal and frequently result in infections and amputations.

Studies estimate that up to 80% of diabetes-related amputations originate from non-healing foot ulcers.

Current treatment strategies rely on off-loading footwear that redistributes pressure away from ulcer sites. However:

  • Existing off-loading shoes often create new pressure points, leading to new ulcers.
  • Many devices are expensive and uncomfortable, resulting in poor patient compliance.
  • Personalized pressure analysis is rarely available in low-resource settings.

AI-driven analysis of gait patterns and plantar pressure data could help identify high-risk pressure zones and guide the design of personalized off-loading solutions.

OBJECTIVE

Develop a machine learning system that predicts high-pressure plantar regions and ulcer risk zones in diabetic patients using gait or plantar pressure data.

The system should identify areas of abnormal pressure distribution that could lead to ulcer formation and provide insights that could guide personalized off-loading footwear design.

ML TASK DEFINITION

Primary Task:  Supervised Machine Learning — Classification / Risk Prediction

Possible formulations:

  1. Pressure hotspot classification — Predict high-risk plantar regions
  2. Ulcer risk prediction — Predict probability of ulcer formation
  3. Plantar pressure map analysis — Detect abnormal pressure distributions

Data modalities may include:

  • Pressure sensor matrices
  • Gait time-series data
  • Foot images or scans
  • Biomechanical features

Expected Output

The system should generate:

• Pressure hotspot detection map

• Ulcer risk score for foot regions

• Visualization of plantar pressure distribution

Dataset:  www.kaggle.com/datasets/khalidsiddiqui2003/dfu-dataset-annotated-into-4-classes

  Problem Statement 2

AI-Based Early Warning System for Patient Physiological Deterioration Using Vital Sign Time-Series Data

PROBLEM CONTEXT

Dependent elderly individuals and patients with neurological conditions such as stroke, dementia, or traumatic brain injury are at high risk of silent physiological decline.

Early warning signs of deterioration—such as infection, dehydration, or systemic instability—often appear first through subtle changes in vital signs including:

  • heart rate
  • respiratory rate
  • temperature
  • blood pressure
  • oxygen saturation

In many cases, these signals are either missed or detected too late, resulting in emergency hospitalization or ICU admission.

Machine learning systems capable of analyzing continuous physiological data streams could help identify early deterioration risk and enable timely intervention by caregivers or clinicians.

Such systems are already being explored in hospitals but remain underdeveloped for home-care and remote monitoring settings.

OBJECTIVE

Develop a machine learning model that predicts the risk of patient physiological deterioration within a defined time window (e.g., next 6–12 hours) using historical vital sign measurements.

The system should analyze time-series patient data and generate a risk score or classification indicating whether the patient is likely to deteriorate.

Participants should also build a simple interface that visualizes patient data and displays the predicted risk level.

ML TASK DEFINITION

Primary ML Task:  Time-Series Prediction / Binary Classification

Predict whether a patient will experience clinical deterioration within the next time window based on previous vital signs.

Possible prediction targets:

  • ICU transfer
  • Sepsis onset
  • Clinical instability
  • Mortality risk

Model Inputs:

  • Time-series vital signs
  • Demographics
  • Clinical measurements

Possible ML approaches:

  • Gradient Boosting (XGBoost / LightGBM)
  • LSTM / GRU models
  • Temporal CNNs
  • Transformer-based time-series models


Expected Deliverables

Teams must submit:

  1. Machine Learning Model
  • trained deterioration prediction model
  1. Data Processing Pipeline
  • preprocessing and feature engineering
  1. Prototype Interface
  • dashboard or web app for predictions
  1. Demo
  • demonstration of system predicting risk
  1. Technical Report

     covering:
  • model architecture
  • dataset used
  • evaluation results
  • limitations

Dataset:  www.kaggle.com/datasets/tarekmasryo/hospital-deterioration-dataset

  Problem Statement 3

AI-Based Osteoporosis Risk Screening from Routine X-Ray Radiographs

PROBLEM CONTEXT

Osteoporosis is a chronic skeletal disease characterized by low bone mineral density (BMD) and deterioration of bone structure, leading to an increased risk of fractures.

Globally, osteoporosis affects hundreds of millions of people, particularly:

  • Postmenopausal women
  • Elderly populations
  • Patients undergoing long-term steroid therapy

The gold standard for diagnosing osteoporosis is DEXA (Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry) scanning. However:

  • DEXA machines are expensive
  • Access is limited in rural and low-resource settings
  • Many hospitals do not routinely perform DEXA screening

X-ray imaging is widely available across healthcare systems.

If AI models could analyze routine radiographs (e.g., wrist, hip, or spine X-rays) to estimate osteoporosis risk, healthcare providers could enable large-scale early screening using existing imaging infrastructure.

Such systems could help identify high-risk patients who should undergo confirmatory DEXA scans.

OBJECTIVE

Develop a machine learning model that analyzes bone radiographs and predicts the risk of osteoporosis or low bone mineral density.

Teams should build a prototype system that:

  • Accepts an X-ray image of a bone region (e.g., wrist or hand)
  • Processes the image using computer vision models
  • Outputs a predicted osteoporosis risk score or classification

The system should demonstrate the feasibility of AI-assisted osteoporosis screening using standard X-ray imaging.

ML TASK DEFINITION

Primary Task:  Computer Vision — Image Classification

Possible formulations:

  1. Binary Classification — Normal bone density / Osteoporosis risk
  2. Multi-class Classification — Normal / Osteopenia / Osteoporosis
  3. Regression (advanced teams) — Predict estimated Bone Mineral Density (BMD)

Expected Deliverables

Each team should submit:

  1.  Trained ML model
  • Image-based prediction model

2. Prototype system

Example:

  • Web interface
  • Image upload + risk prediction

3. Technical documentation

Including:

  • Model architecture
  • Data preprocessing
  • Training pipeline
  • Evaluation results

4. Demonstration

Live or recorded demo showing:

  • Upload X-ray
  • Receive prediction

Dataset:  www.kaggle.com/datasets/kmader/rsna-bone-age

  Problem Statement 4

AI-Based Detection of Incorrect Exercise Form Using Human Pose Estimation

PROBLEM CONTEXT

Incorrect exercise form is a leading contributor to musculoskeletal injuries in both fitness and rehabilitation settings.

Poor movement patterns such as:

  • joint misalignment
  • asymmetrical motion
  • compensatory movement
  • excessive joint angles

can place abnormal stress on joints including the knees, hips, shoulders, and spine, increasing the risk of chronic injuries.

In clinical settings, physiotherapists and trainers assess movement quality visually, identifying incorrect biomechanics during exercises like squats, lunges, and shoulder movements.

However, most individuals exercise without professional supervision, making it difficult to detect poor form early.

Advances in computer vision and pose estimation now allow smartphones and cameras to capture human motion and estimate skeletal joint positions, enabling automated biomechanical analysis.

An AI system capable of detecting incorrect movement patterns could provide real-time feedback and injury prevention support for both fitness and rehabilitation applications.

OBJECTIVE

Develop a machine learning system that analyzes human exercise movements using pose estimation and detects incorrect exercise form or biomechanical abnormalities.

The system should:

  1. Extract human pose keypoints from exercise images or videos.
  2. Analyze joint angles and movement patterns.
  3. Classify whether the exercise form is: Correct or Incorrect (with possible error categories).

The final prototype should include a simple interface where users upload a short exercise video or image sequence and receive feedback on form quality.

ML TASK DEFINITION

Primary Task:  Computer Vision + Classification

Pipeline structure:

  1. Pose Estimation — Extract skeletal joint coordinates from images/video frames.
  2. Feature Engineering — Joint angles, joint trajectories, symmetry metrics, movement velocity.
  3. ML Classification

Possible labels:

  • Correct form
  • Incorrect form

Optional advanced labels:

  • Knee valgus
  • Asymmetrical squat
  • Insufficient depth
  • Forward trunk lean

Expected Deliverables

Teams should submit:

  1. Trained ML model
  2. Pose estimation pipeline
  3. Exercise form classification model
  4. Prototype interface

Example interfaces:

  • Web app
  • Mobile-friendly web tool
  • Video upload interface
  1. Technical documentation

Including:

  • Dataset used
  • Feature engineering
  • Model architecture
  • Evaluation results
  1. Final demo

Demonstrating the system analyzing exercise movement.

Datasets:  kaggle.com/datasets/matthewjansen/ucf101-action-recognition   |   crcv.ucf.edu/research/data-sets/ucf101

  Problem Statement 5

AI-Based Infarct Volume Estimation from Non-Contrast CT for Acute Anterior Circulation Stroke

PROBLEM CONTEXT

Acute ischemic stroke is one of the leading causes of death and disability worldwide. Rapid identification of infarct size is critical for determining eligibility for treatments such as mechanical thrombectomy and thrombolysis.

In clinical practice:

  • Non-contrast CT (NCCT) is the most widely available emergency imaging modality.
  • However, early ischemic changes on NCCT can be extremely subtle, making accurate estimation of infarct volume challenging even for experienced radiologists.

Advanced imaging methods such as CT perfusion or MRI provide better infarct assessment but are not always available in emergency settings.

An AI system capable of estimating infarct volume directly from standard NCCT scans could support faster triage decisions, particularly in resource-limited hospitals.

OBJECTIVE

Develop a machine learning system that analyzes non-contrast CT brain scans and estimates the ischemic stroke lesion volume in patients with anterior circulation acute ischemic stroke.

The system should automatically detect and segment infarct regions and compute an estimated infarct volume.

ML TASK DEFINITION

Primary Task:  Medical image segmentation

  • Input: Non-contrast CT brain scan
  • Output: Pixel/voxel-level infarct segmentation mask

Secondary Task:  Regression / Volume estimation

  • Compute predicted infarct volume (mL)

Optional extension:

Classify infarct size:

  • Small (<30 mL)
  • Medium (30–70 mL)
  • Large (>70 mL)

Expected Output

Teams should develop a system that:

  1. Accepts a non-contrast CT brain scan
  2. Identifies and segments suspected ischemic regions
  3. Calculates estimated infarct volume
  4. Displays results via a simple interface

Example output:

  • Infarct segmentation overlay on CT slices
  • Estimated infarct volume (mL)
  • Optional infarct severity category

Dataset:  www.kaggle.com/datasets/orvile/cpaisd-acute-ischemic-stroke-dataset
